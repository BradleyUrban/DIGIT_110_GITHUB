Inside the Void

Game Analysis/Review

Description

  • Alien structures have been sighted on a remote planet. Several spacecraft have been sent to investigate the structures, but all communication with the crew has been lost. Your mission is to search for the lost crew.
  • Inside the Void is an exploration game where you must navigate through seven alien structures in an effort to unravel the disappearance of lost crew members. You will encounter messages left by the lost crew members that offer a glimpse into their fate.

  • Seeing the background of the game being the 3D shapes and archetype is the core desgin of the game and its setting.

Interaction

  • After you land I noticed there were little sphereical podeiums around the place, which I soon found out to be entry logs.
  • You can interact with these node that are misson logs on past crewmates.
  • Only problem is you can only interact in this game, there is no inventor.
  • Meaning if you want to see those past logs again you have to physically go back to them which is a problem for some logs.
  • There is a border to the game, but it looks and feels like you could journey out into the void forever.
  • You then see in the distance in all directions these giant 3D shapes hovering over top of the world, which later you figure they are alien spaceships.

Overall and Notes

  • Going on to the Main person you play as, we don't have a name of this person, nor do we see what they look like.
  • So, you only play as this mystery person, and there are also no NPC's in the game too.
  • Menaing the only way to lnow what actually happend is to read all of logs, which you should do anyway.
  • Going through most of the gameplay/game I can tell your suppose to visit all the giant spaceships scattered around the map, and find all the entry logs to see what exactly happened to the crewmates.
  • As a gamer and in my experience, I thought it was alright and wasn't difficult to pick up on what the author was conveying.
  • Overall, it was a pretty cool game and defiently needs some work on it for me to come back to it, but visualy enjoyoing too.
